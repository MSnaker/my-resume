I am Marco Bissaro, I graduated in October 2020 from my MSc. in Aerospace Engineering at University of Padua. Other than the main subjects in my study curriculum, I also approached Elements of Automatic Controls, Electrical actuators, and Vibrational mechanics. After my studies I have been working in process automation, first in the Machining Department of a foundry, then in a Satellite Design and Manufacturing company, focused mainly on the New Space.

What my curriculum doesn't show is the pleasure I have always found in learning: I can proudly say that I have enjoyed all and every one of the subjects I faced during my studies: although they might have been at some times difficult, I never really felt any weight associated with them.
This advantage perhaps stems from my curiosity, which I deem my best and most defining quality. Evidence of this is my focus that, after the scientific studies, moved to literary and artistic matters (history, classic and foreign literature); nothing, however, prevents all natural phenomena I see from forcing me to consider their physical causes.

I recently discovered a new pleasure in DIY by doing some customisations to my studying/gaming setup: I added a physical switch to my PC to switch the input between two Hard Disks (one dedicated to study/work, the other to games and entertainment), and I am constantly designing objects to ease my life at home (mostly 3D-printed, but I dared with a sheet metal object, once, and it paid off).
